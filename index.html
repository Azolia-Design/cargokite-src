Problems

The world is depending on a struggling shipping industry.

No resilience

One single incident, such as a ship getting stuck in the Suez Canal or one labor strike in the port of Long Beach shuts down major parts of global shipping causing $17 billion damages in a few days for global supply chains.

Massive pollution

Global shipping is responsible for almost 1 Gigatonne of CO2 emissions every year - that's more than all of Germany combined!

No suitable
for small trades

Large ships are very cost efficient, but only 20 ports have the infrastructure for large vessels and many routes don't have enough transportation demand to fill large ships.

Huge inventory

Having to serve thousands of customers with a single ship makes individualized, just-in-time delivery impossible for shipping companies, causing them to have to build up unnecessary inventory.

Long transport times

98% of ports are not directly connected making requiring time intensive transshipments of containers & stops of ships operating on round trips instead of point to point transport all at the cost of long transport times.

Solutions

Micro-Ship
is our sustainable answer

We are building the sailing ship of the 21st century. Our innovative concept utilizes high altitude winds as its only means of propulsion with efficiency that has never been seen before.

In effect, we offer a fully sustainable option to transport goods that is efficient at the same time.

Small ships for flexibility

Our ships are designed to be comparatively small. In effect supply chain flexibility and resilience become possible again.
